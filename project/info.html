Traditional learning and corporate training have always revolved around gathering students together, in one space, and talking at them before quizzing them to see how much information was absorbed. The rise of Electronic Learning, or the e-Learning system, broke down some of those boundaries: it helped make learning more interactive and also allowed learners to learn off-site in remote locations. However, eLearning has advanced in recent years to the point where, thanks to the rise of the smartphone and almost total, market saturation, we now have mobile learning at the forefront of the field.
